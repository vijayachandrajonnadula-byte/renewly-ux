# Problem Statement

## Context

Renewly is a mock-data UX/UI portfolio project for a B2B SaaS subscription and renewal management platform. The product concept focuses on helping teams understand which software subscriptions are renewing soon, who owns each decision, what risk is attached, and where savings may be possible.

## Problem

SaaS renewal work is often spread across spreadsheets, inboxes, calendar reminders, contract folders, and informal owner knowledge. When renewal context is fragmented, teams can struggle to answer basic questions quickly:

- Which renewals need attention first?
- Who owns the decision?
- Is the product being used enough to justify renewal?
- Are there duplicate or overlapping tools?
- Which approvals are still pending?
- Where might renegotiation or cancellation create savings?

This project does not claim real user research or real customer validation. The problem framing is based on a common B2B operations pattern and will be explored through mock data, product-design reasoning, and portfolio documentation.

## User Pain Points

- Renewal dates can be missed when ownership and reminders are unclear.
- Finance and procurement teams may lack usage context when reviewing spend.
- Department owners may not see all information needed to make a decision.
- Approval status can become hard to track across email and chat.
- Savings opportunities may stay hidden until it is too late to act.

## Design Challenge

Design a focused SaaS experience that helps teams move from scattered renewal information to prioritized, confident action. The interface should reduce decision overload, reveal urgent work, support progressive disclosure, and prevent accidental renewal decisions.

## Success Criteria for the Portfolio Project

- The problem is understandable without relying on invented statistics.
- Key workflows are framed around realistic B2B renewal tasks.
- Documentation shows clear product thinking before UI implementation.
- Future UI decisions can be traced back to user needs and UX principles.
