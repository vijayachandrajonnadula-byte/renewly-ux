# Product Bet

## Bet Statement

If renewal teams can see upcoming subscriptions grouped by urgency, owner, approval status, usage signal, risk, and savings potential, then they can make faster and more confident renewal decisions with less manual coordination.

## Why This Bet Matters

Renewal decisions involve unavoidable complexity: contracts, owners, deadlines, spend, usage, business value, approval steps, and risk. Renewly does not try to remove that complexity entirely. Instead, it aims to structure it so users can focus on the next best decision.

## Primary Value Proposition

Renewly gives finance, procurement, IT, and department stakeholders a shared place to review renewal work before it becomes urgent.

## Product Principles

- Prioritize the next decision over showing every possible data point at once.
- Make risk and savings opportunities visually distinct.
- Keep ownership and approval state visible.
- Show summaries first, then allow deeper inspection.
- Use familiar SaaS dashboard patterns so users can orient quickly.
- Add confirmation steps for high-impact decisions.

## Assumptions to Explore

- Teams need a clear renewal queue more than a broad analytics dashboard.
- Renewal urgency is easier to act on when grouped by time window and risk.
- Owners need enough context to decide without reading every contract detail.
- Savings opportunities are more useful when attached to a specific action.
- Approval visibility can reduce repeated follow-up across communication tools.

## Portfolio Constraint

This is a mock-data concept. The product bet should be presented as a design hypothesis, not as a validated business claim.
