# Product Bet

## Main Product Bet

If Renewly organizes subscription renewals by urgency, owner, usage context, approval status, renewal risk, and estimated savings opportunity, then users can make faster and more confident renewal decisions without relying on scattered spreadsheets, inboxes, and memory.

The bet is not that Renewly can remove all renewal complexity. The bet is that a focused workflow can structure that complexity so users know what needs attention, what information is missing, and what decision should happen next.

## Supporting Assumptions

- Users need a prioritized renewal workflow more than a broad software inventory as the first experience.
- Renewal decisions become easier when deadline, owner, usage, risk, and approval state are visible together.
- Users are more likely to act on savings opportunities when they are attached to a specific subscription and next step.
- Pending approvals should remain visible until resolved because incomplete reviews are easy to lose across communication tools.
- High-risk renewals need stronger visual treatment than ordinary renewals.
- Users need confirmation before approving, canceling, or escalating high-impact subscription decisions.
- Mock data can demonstrate the product logic clearly without claiming real integrations or live analytics.

## What the Product Should Help Users Decide

Renewly should help users answer:

- Which renewals need attention first?
- Who owns the tool and who needs to approve the decision?
- Is the renewal routine, risky, or potentially wasteful?
- Is usage strong enough to support renewal?
- Is there enough information to approve the renewal now?
- Should the team renew, renegotiate, cancel, consolidate, or request more information?
- Which pending approvals still need follow-up?
- What estimated savings opportunities should be reviewed before the renewal deadline?

## Experience Principles

- Use Hick's Law to narrow decisions to the most relevant next actions.
- Use Jakob's Law by following familiar SaaS navigation, dashboard, table, and detail-page conventions.
- Use Miller's Law and Law of Common Region to organize dense renewal data into clear sections.
- Use Law of Proximity to keep related facts close to the decision they support.
- Use Von Restorff Effect to draw attention to risk and savings exceptions.
- Use Zeigarnik Effect to make pending approvals and incomplete reviews visible.
- Use Progressive Disclosure to avoid overwhelming users before they need detail.
- Use Recognition over Recall by showing deadlines, owners, statuses, and risk labels directly.
- Use Error Prevention for approval, cancellation, and other high-impact choices.
