# Case Study Draft

## Project Title

Renewly: B2B SaaS Renewal Management Dashboard

## One-Line Summary

Renewly is a mock-data product design portfolio project that explores how teams can track software renewals, ownership, risk, approvals, and savings opportunities in one focused SaaS workspace.

## Role

Product design and frontend implementation for a portfolio concept.

## Project Type

Mock-data UX/UI portfolio project.

## Problem

Software renewal decisions often require information from finance, procurement, IT, and department owners. When renewal dates, owners, usage context, approval status, and savings opportunities are scattered, teams may review too late or make decisions without enough context.

## Product Hypothesis

Teams can make better renewal decisions when the product organizes renewal work by urgency, owner, usage signal, approval state, risk, and savings opportunity.

## Design Approach

The project will begin with product framing, assumptions, provisional personas, user journey mapping, information architecture, wireframe planning, visual direction, and UX decision logging before UI implementation.

## UX Principles Used

- Hick's Law to reduce decision overload.
- Jakob's Law to use familiar SaaS patterns.
- Miller's Law to group complex information.
- Von Restorff Effect to highlight high-risk renewals and savings opportunities.
- Zeigarnik Effect to keep pending approvals visible.
- Progressive Disclosure to show summary first and details second.
- Error Prevention to reduce accidental renewal decisions.
- Peak-End Rule to make confirmations clear and reassuring.

## Proposed Core Experience

1. Dashboard overview of renewal workload.
2. Prioritized renewal queue.
3. Subscription detail page with decision context.
4. Guided decision actions.
5. Confirmation summary.
6. Updated approval and renewal status.

## Constraints

- No real integrations.
- No real customer data.
- No claimed user testing.
- Mock data only.
- Frontend-first portfolio implementation.
