# Information Architecture

## Product Areas

### Dashboard

Purpose: Provide a summary of renewal work and direct users to the most important next actions.

Core content:

- Upcoming renewals.
- High-risk renewals.
- Pending approvals.
- Estimated savings opportunities.
- Recently completed decisions.

### Renewal Queue

Purpose: Help users prioritize and act on subscriptions that need review.

Core content:

- Subscription name.
- Vendor/category.
- Renewal date.
- Owner.
- Spend level.
- Usage signal.
- Risk level.
- Approval status.
- Recommended next step.

### Subscription Detail

Purpose: Give users enough context to make or route a renewal decision.

Core content:

- Summary facts.
- Renewal timeline.
- Owner and approver information.
- Usage and adoption signals.
- Spend and savings estimate.
- Notes and decision history.
- Guided decision actions.

### Approvals

Purpose: Make unresolved decisions visible and easy to follow up on.

Core content:

- Pending approval list.
- Approver name or role.
- Due date.
- Decision status.
- Requested action.

### Savings Opportunities

Purpose: Highlight possible cost improvements without turning the product into a generic analytics dashboard.

Core content:

- Subscription or vendor.
- Opportunity type.
- Estimated savings range or qualitative indicator.
- Supporting signal.
- Suggested action.

## Navigation Model

Initial navigation should stay simple:

- Dashboard.
- Renewals.
- Approvals.
- Opportunities.
- Settings or mock account area if needed later.

## IA Principles

- Keep dashboard content summary-level.
- Move detailed context into subscription detail views.
- Use clear status labels and avoid hidden decision states.
- Group renewal data by task rather than by internal data model.
- Preserve familiar B2B SaaS navigation patterns.

## Future IA Questions

- Should savings opportunities be a separate view or a filtered renewal queue?
- Should approvals live as their own page or as a dashboard module first?
- How much mock usage data is enough to support decisions without overloading the interface?
