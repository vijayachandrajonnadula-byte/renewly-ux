# Research Notes

## Research Status

No real user testing, interviews, analytics, or customer research have been conducted for this project. These notes are a planning scaffold for future research and a way to document product assumptions responsibly.

## Research Goals

- Understand how teams currently track software renewals.
- Identify where renewal decisions slow down or become risky.
- Learn what information finance, procurement, IT, and department owners need before approving a renewal.
- Explore how teams define renewal risk and savings opportunity.
- Understand which confirmation steps help prevent accidental decisions.

## Proto-Research Questions

- How do teams currently know which software is renewing soon?
- Who is responsible for making or approving each renewal decision?
- What information is missing when a renewal review begins?
- Which signals indicate that a subscription may be high risk?
- Which signals indicate a possible savings opportunity?
- How are renewal decisions recorded and communicated today?
- What makes a renewal workflow feel trustworthy?

## Assumptions to Validate Later

- Users want a prioritized queue instead of searching through a full inventory first.
- Usage and owner context are more actionable when placed near renewal deadlines.
- Pending approvals should remain visible until resolved.
- High-risk renewals need stronger visual treatment than ordinary items.
- Users need confirmation moments for irreversible or expensive decisions.

## Research Ethics and Portfolio Notes

- Do not present assumptions as findings.
- Do not invent sample sizes, survey results, or interview quotes.
- Clearly label future research opportunities.
- Use mock data only for demonstration.

## Potential Future Methods

- Stakeholder interviews with finance, procurement, IT, and department leads.
- Usability testing of the renewal queue and subscription detail workflow.
- Card sorting for information architecture.
- Preference testing for risk and savings indicators.
- Task-based testing for approval and confirmation flows.
