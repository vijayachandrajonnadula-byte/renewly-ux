# Personas

## Persona Status

These are provisional personas for a mock-data portfolio project. They are based on plausible B2B SaaS renewal roles, not direct research interviews.

## Primary Persona: Finance Operations Manager

### Goals

- Keep software spend predictable.
- Identify upcoming renewals before deadlines.
- Spot savings opportunities and duplicate tools.
- Understand approval status without chasing stakeholders.

### Needs

- Renewal queue sorted by urgency and impact.
- Clear subscription owner and approver information.
- Estimated spend and savings context.
- Status visibility for pending reviews.

### Frictions

- Renewal context is scattered across tools.
- Owners may respond late.
- Usage context may be unavailable when spend is reviewed.
- High-impact decisions can be buried in a long list.

## Secondary Persona: IT Operations Lead

### Goals

- Maintain visibility into software ownership and usage.
- Reduce unused or risky tools.
- Support renewal decisions with operational context.

### Needs

- Usage signals and ownership details.
- Clear risk markers.
- Notes on access, usage, and tool overlap.
- A way to flag incomplete information.

### Frictions

- Usage data may be incomplete or inconsistent.
- Tool ownership can change without documentation.
- Technical context may not be visible to finance reviewers.

## Secondary Persona: Department Owner

### Goals

- Keep essential tools available for the team.
- Justify renewals with business value.
- Avoid surprise cancellations or budget issues.

### Needs

- Simple review experience with relevant facts.
- Clear deadline and decision status.
- Easy way to request more information.
- Confirmation before final decisions.

### Frictions

- May not know contract or spend details.
- May receive renewal requests too late.
- May need to coordinate with multiple stakeholders.

## Design Implications

- Use dashboards and queues that support scanning.
- Group information by owner, urgency, spend, usage, and decision state.
- Make pending approvals visible to support follow-through.
- Avoid exposing too many decision paths at once.
